Welcome to your Destinations

Lalibela

Lalibela, located in Ethiopia, is renowned for its rock-hewn churches, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, intricately carved from solid volcanic rock. These awe-inspiring churches, constructed in the 12th century, exhibit remarkable architectural and historical significance, attracting pilgrims and tourists worldwide.

Danakil depresession

The Danakil Depression, situated in Ethiopia, is an otherworldly geological marvel renowned for its extreme heat, salt flats, and colorful mineral deposits. This hostile landscape, one of the hottest and lowest places on Earth, boasts volcanic formations, salt lakes, and geysers, offering a surreal and unique experience for adventurous travelers.

Simien Mountains

The Simien Mountains, found in Ethiopia, stand as a majestic and rugged wonderland, showcasing stunning cliffs, deep valleys, and distinctive wildlife. This UNESCO-listed national park is renowned for its dramatic landscapes, including the towering peaks, deep gorges, and the iconic Ras Dashen, the highest point in Ethiopia.

Fasil Ghebbi

Fasil Ghebbi, located in Gondar, Ethiopia, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an exceptional representation of medieval African architecture and urban planning. This historical complex comprises palaces, churches, libraries, and royal buildings surrounded by a massive stone wall, reflecting the grandeur and cultural significance of the Gondarine period.

Axum

Axum, an ancient city in Ethiopia, boasts millennia-old ruins and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its historical significance and the remnants of the Kingdom of Aksum.

Omo Valley

Omo Valley, nestled in southern Ethiopia, is a cultural melting pot, home to diverse indigenous tribes preserving ancient customs, traditions, and a rich heritage amid breathtaking landscapes.

Bahir Dar

Bahir Dar, with its wide avenues of palms and scenic lakeside location, is one of Ethiopia's most attractive towns. Located on the southern shore of Lake Tana, it is an ideal base from which to explore the lake and surrounding area, which includes the blue nile falls.

Tiya & melka kuntare

an archaeological site, holds stone tools and artifacts dating back millions of years, providing insight into early human history and evolution in the region.

Harar

Harar, known for its fortified walls, alleys, and vibrant markets, houses the historic Grand Jami Mosque, showcasing exceptional Islamic architecture, adding to the city's cultural charm and significance in Ethiopia.

Bale Mountains National Park

Bale Mountains National Park in Ethiopia is a diverse haven, home to unique wildlife like the Ethiopian wolf and mountain nyala, boasting Afroalpine landscapes, lush forests, and vast grasslands, offering a rich biodiversity and stunning natural beauty.

Turmi

This traditional town, not far from the Kenyan border, lies right down in the heart of the south Omo region and is an important transport hub, located at a three-way road intersection. It is the main town of the Hamer people and is known throughout the Omo Valley for its colourful Monday market, one of the most important in Hamer country.
